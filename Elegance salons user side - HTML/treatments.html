+Threading

Threading

Threading is an ancient technique originating from the Middle East and South Asia, for eyebrow shaping and other areas of hair removal for peach fuzz free skin leading to finer, softer and smoother regrowth. It provides precise control for shaping perfect eyebrows and is gentle on skin, giving you a smooth finish for up to five weeks. This technique has proven great results, alternative to waxing and plucking. However, there are still many people that do not know about threading and how it can improve their beauty regime.

Benefits of threading

Unlike other hair removal methods, threading does not use chemicals and is safe, therefore it minimises the risks of irritation. If you are prone to skin sensitivity, itchiness or redness this technique of hair removal is beneficial for you. This method of removal is accurate, individual hairs can be targeted but multiple hair can be targeted removing one clean line of hair at once. As threading weakens the hair follicle it causes the growth to be less dense over time providing longer lasting results.

1.FAQ
1. How is threading done?
A. Threading is done using a great technique of using a special 100% sterile thread, which is double-twisted and glided along the skin to gently remove unwanted hair from the root.

2. Is there anything I need to do before my threading treatment? Threading Pre-care?
A. We recommend that you exfoliate the area to be threaded at least three days before your treatment, this allows any growth which is still visible but beneath the skins surface to be threaded for a clean finish. To achieve the best results avoid plucking and waxing, this allows us to catch hair growth at all stages in one go until your next visit! Try to avoid make up on the area, this makes the threading process easier and more precise.

3. How long does threading last?
A. Threading can generally last up to 3-4 weeks, depending on your hair type and area of the face treated. Full hair growth can be from 2-5 weeks.

4. Is threading painful?
A. Threading is gentle on the skin, but our pain tolerance varies. Most of our clients tell us they feel a slight discomfort during the process, and some tell us there is no discomfort at all!

5. How old must I be to have this done?
A. From age of 11+ you can receive this service with consent of parent or guardian.

5. Can I thread more than just my eyebrows?
A. Threading can be done on any part of the face, from upper lip, chin and side of face.

6. I am using Retin A/Accutane, is threading suitable for me?
A. Threading is your best choice of hair removal as taking these medications can cause your skin to become thin and sensitive. Dermatologists also approve of threading, as this does not break your skin, nor pull it like waxing methods. Threading is a great method for all skin types and a safe process to carry out.

7. Are there any possibilities of cross contamination with threading?
A. With this method we use special 100% sterile thread, which is glided over the area. Once your session has ended the thread used is disposed of straight away, therefore you never have to worry about passing bacteria from one person to another.

8. What should I be doing after my treatment, are there any restrictions? /Threading aftercare
A. We recommend washing your face with cold water after your threading, this minimises post treatment redness and apply Aloe Vera gel to soothe the area treated. If you experience irritation or red bumps on your skin at times this is normal as it’s our body’s natural way of protecting us, rub some ice cubes on the area for a couple of minutes. For at least 24 hours after your treatment, avoid perfumed products, direct sunlight/fake tan, chlorinated water, and steam as the area will be highly sensitive. Exposure mentioned can cause irritation, sunburn and inflammation.

+Tinting

Tinting

If you are fed up of filling your eyebrows every day or have fair coloured lashes and want to avoid applying mascara, our tinting treatment is suitable for you. Tinting is a completely painless semi-permanent colour option which gives you a long lasting, natural and a fuller look to your brows and lashes.

Benefits of Tinting

Tinting is a quick procedure is suitable for you if you want to emphasise your brows and lashes giving them a naturally darker fuller look, or if you are allergic to mascara or have make up issues and want to save time in the morning in having to do this all.

Get in touch with the team at your local Elegance Beauty salon for more information or book your threading appointment online today!

1.FAQ
1. How is tinting done?
A. Tinting is similar as having your hair dyed. Firstly, petroleum jelly is applied to the skin around the area to stop the dye from staining your skin. A semi-permanent dye is custom mixed to get the perfect shade for you. The tint is then left on for different amounts of time to achieve the right shade. This process can take up to 5-10 minutes depending on your hair type.

2. Is there anything I need to do before my tinting treatment? Tinting Pre-care?
A. Firstly, please make sure you are aware of the patch test requirements. If you are having your eyelashes tinted and wear contact lenses, bring your solution and case with you to pop your contacts in for the time you are having the tinting treatment. Make sure you have clean lashes and brows free from any residue from any oils and tanning products. This will allow your tint to absorb properly.

3. How long does tinting last?
A. Tinting can usually last up to 3-4 weeks, this varies from person to person depending on your skincare routine, skin type and your exposure to the sun which fades the pigment quickly. The tint fades away naturally and we recommend a touch up every 4 weeks.

4. How old must I be to have this done?
A. From age of 16+ you can receive this service.

5. Can I tint more than just my eyebrows?
A. Tinting can be done on your eyebrows and eyelashes only.

5. Can I choose which colour I want? I don’t want to go too dark!
A. Please let your therapist know what results you are looking for. Our therapists will offer the best advice on what shade of tint to opt for, best suiting your hair and skin tone. Ideally you should opt for the colour to be 1-2 shades lighter/darker than your hair colour.

6. Do I need a patch test for tinting?
A. As our client your health & safety is our top priority. Yes, you require a patch test 48 hours prior to the treatment, if you haven’t had the tint treatment carried out from our salons before or within the last 6 months and no changes in your medical history. The patch test involves having a small amount of dye applied behind your ear, this process only takes seconds to do this. Please don’t get annoyed if you haven’t factored this in time, you may have seen horrific things happening, therefore this cannot be skipped. You can pop into any of your nearest elegance salons/bars at any time to have this done.

7. I am pregnant, is this suitable for me?
A. We encourage clients to avoid tinting during the time of pregnancy, as a reaction from the tint is more likely to occur due to hormonal changes. If this did happen, you would not be able to take medication due to pregnancy.

8. What should I be doing after my treatment, are there any restrictions?
A. Tinting aftercare
  • Avoid eye make up for 12 hours
  • Avoid/touching or rubbing your eyes straight after the treatment
  • For 24 hours avoid sauna, swimming, and sunbeds
  • Although we do make sure every trace of dye is removed, it is possible that residue may still remain
  • Use oil free based products around the eye area to prolong the colour
  • If any redness or irritation occurs, apply a damp cotton wool compress. If symptoms persist more than 24 hours seek medical advice

+Eyebrow Waxing

Waxing

Waxing is a safe method to temporarily remove unwanted hair for up to 6 weeks. Waxing produces smoother results as it removes hair from the roots resulting in slower regrowth, unlike shaving which the hair grows back within days.

What are the benefits of Waxing?

Waxing slows down re-growth and you may find the hair to be finer. As there are minimal chemicals used in waxing, this rarely causes skin irritation and allergies. As you may know shaving causes the skin to be rough and stubbly, waxing removes the hair from the root providing smooth exfoliated skin and longer lasting results.

Get in touch with the team at your local Elegance Beauty salon for more information or book your threading appointment online today!

1.FAQ
1. How is Waxing done?
A. Waxing is done by using two types of methods, which is warm wax and hot wax. A paper strip is applied and pressed firmly, adhering the strip to the wax it is then pulled off against the direction of the hair growth. The hot wax method is done the same way, but without the use of a paper strips, the wax is self-setting and pulled off against the direction of the hair growth.

2. Is there anything I need to do before my waxing treatment? Waxing Pre-care?
A. We recommend you exfoliate the day before your waxing appointment to soften your skin and prevent ingrown hair. Please do not apply any moisturisers or lotions on the day of your treatment. If you are having under arm waxing, try to use a deodorant with anti-perspirant this allows the hair to be removed easily. Bath or shower on the day prior to your treatment, to open up follicles allowing easy hair removal.

3. How long does my hair need to be?
A. For an easy and safe waxing treatment, the hair must be ¼ inches long. If the hair is too short it will be difficult to remove, also if you are shaving for a method of quick hair removal please allow 10 days for regrowth to do a waxing treatment.

4. How long do waxing results last?
A. The results will vary person to person, and on your hair growth cycle. Typically, you can expect the results to last anywhere between 3-5 weeks. You will see a longer lasting effect with repeat treatments. We recommend waxing treatments to be done every 4 weeks.

5. Will my hair re-growth be thicker with waxing treatments?
A. No, with consistent waxing treatments you will notice your hair re-growth to become finer and slower.

5. I’m pregnant, is this suitable for me?
A. Yes, you can have waxing treatments done. If you are having an intimate waxing treatment, we recommend you have this done after your first trimester.

6. Is waxing safe for sensitive skin?
A. Waxing is much gentler on the skin compared to other methods such as shaving. If you have sensitive skin, it’s very important to follow the pre/aftercare instructions provided. We also do laser hair removal as another safe option, which also lasts longer between 3-4 years with maintenance, why not speak to our technicians for further advice!

7. I have the following conditions/taking medication or have had treatments done, is this suitable for me?
A.
  • Active herpes outbreak
  • Sunburn on the area to be treated
  • Chemical peel within the last 10 days
  • Use of retinol on the area to be treated within last 3 days
  • Acne medication such as Accutane or within the last 6 months
  • Eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis or varicose veins in the area to be treated
Unfortunately, we will not be able to carry out your waxing treatment with the above.

8. What should I be doing after my treatment, are there any restrictions? /Waxing aftercare
A. We recommend you wear clean, lose fit clothing after your waxing treatment, and apply the products of which your therapist will let you know about.

For at least 24 hours after your treatment, avoid perfumed products/deodorants, direct sunlight/fake tan, chlorinated water, steam, make up, rubbing or scratching the area as your skin will be highly sensitive. Avoid exercising for 24 hours as sweating may cause the skin to be itchy. Exposure mentioned can cause irritation and inflammation. If any irritation occurs apply a cool damp compress to the area, if these symptoms persist more than 24 hours, seek medical advice.

+Strip Lashes

Strip Lashes

Strip lashes come in the form of a natural or a dramatic look. These lashes are attached to a thin strip that adheres to your eyelid.

What are the benefits of Strip Lashes?

This treatment is a quick process if you want lashes for a day’s occasion or night out. Strips lashes can be reused if they are looked after well and removed carefully. With these lashes you can achieve various looks.

Get in touch with the team at your local Elegance Beauty salon for more information or book your threading appointment online today!

1.FAQ
1. Is there anything I need to do before my lash treatment? Pre-care?
A.
  • If you wear waterproof mascara avoid use for 3 days prior to your appointment
  • Make sure your eyes and lashes are clean
  • Avoid oil-based products around the eyes
  • Shower before your appointment as you will not be allowed to get the lashes wet for 24 hours after your treatment
  • Remove contact lenses during the appointment-bring your case and solution with you
.

2. How long do Strip Lashes last for?
A. Ideally, they can last 1-2 days if looked after well. You can remove them carefully and reapply them the next day.

3. How long will this treatment take?
A. Your appointment will take approximately 10-15 minutes.

4. Do I need a patch test for this treatment?
A. You do not require a patch test for strip lashes. However, if you are allergic to latex please let the therapist know so as they can recommend an alternative treatment for you.

5. Will this hurt?
A. No, this is an eye closed treatment you will find it relaxing.

5. How will the strip lashes feel on me?
A. Once the glue has adhered and dried, you should not feel any discomfort at all.

6. Can I apply mascara on my false lashes?
A. Applying mascara can give a dramatic look, however if you do not apply any products on your strip lashes you can get longer out of them.

7. Can I get the lashes wet?
A. Try not to, Once the lashes get wet, it will be hard to maintain its curl and shape.

8. How can I remove these lashes?
A. Apply some make up remover with a cotton bud on the lash line, uses tweezers to peel them off.

9. What should I be doing after my treatment, are there any restrictions? Aftercare
A.
  • For 48 hours after application do not swim, steam, or use sauna
  • Do not apply waterproof mascara as the remover for this will affect the bonding of the lashes
  • Do not use lash curlers as this could break the lash bonding
  • Never pull lashes as this will also cause your natural lashes to fall out
  • Be gentle with the false lashes, avoid tugging and getting them caught of clothes and towels

A paper strip is applied and pressed firmly, adhering the strip to the wax it is then pulled off against the direction of the hair growth. The hot wax method is done the same way, but without the use of a paper strips, the wax is self-setting and pulled off against the direction of the hair growth.

2. Is there anything I need to do before my waxing treatment? Waxing Pre-care?
A. We recommend you exfoliate the day before your waxing appointment to soften your skin and prevent ingrown hair. Please do not apply any moisturisers or lotions on the day of your treatment. If you are having under arm waxing, try to use a deodorant with anti-perspirant this allows the hair to be removed easily. Bath or shower on the day prior to your treatment, to open up follicles allowing easy hair removal.

3. How old must I be to have this done?
A. From age of 16+ you can receive this service.

4. How long does my hair need to be?
A. For an easy and safe waxing treatment, the hair must be ¼ inches long. If the hair is too short it will be difficult to remove, also if you are shaving for a method of quick hair removal please allow 10 days for regrowth to do a waxing treatment.

5. How long do waxing results last?
A. The results will vary person to person, and on your hair growth cycle. Typically, you can expect the results to last anywhere between 3-5 weeks. You will see a longer lasting effect with with repeat treatments. We recommend waxing treatments to be done every 4 weeks.

6. Will my hair re-growth be thicker with waxing treatments?
A. No, with consistent waxing treatments you will notice your hair re-growth to become finer and slower.

7. I’m pregnant, is this suitable for me?
A. Yes, you can have waxing treatments done. If you are having an intimate waxing treatment, we recommend you have this done after your first trimester.

8. Is waxing safe for sensitive skin?

We also do laser hair removal as another safe option, which also lasts longer between 3-4 years with maintenance, why not speak to our technicians for further advice!

9. I have the following conditions/taking medication or have had treatments done, is this suitable for me?
A.
  • Active herpes outbreak
  • Sunburn on the area to be treated
  • Chemical peel within the last 10 days
  • Use of retinol on the area to be treated within last 3 days
  • Acne medication such as Accutane or within the last 6 months
  • Eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis or varicose veins in the area to be treated
Unfortunately, we will not be able to carry out your waxing treatment with the above.

10. What should I be doing after my treatment, are there any restrictions? Waxing aftercare
A. We recommend you wear clean, lose fit clothing after your waxing treatment, and apply the products of which your therapist will let you know about.

Avoid exercising for 24 hours as sweating may cause the skin to be itchy. Exposure mentioned can cause irritation and inflammation. If any irritation occurs apply a cool damp compress to the area, if these symptoms persist more than 24 hours, seek medical advice.

1. How is Waxing done?
A. Waxing is done by using two types of methods, which is warm wax and hot wax. A paper strip is applied and pressed firmly, adhering the strip to the wax it is then pulled off against the direction of the hair growth. The hot wax method is done the same way, but without the use of a paper strips, the wax is self-setting and pulled off against the direction of the hair growth.

2. Is there anything I need to do before my waxing treatment? Waxing Pre-care?
A. We recommend you exfoliate the day before your waxing appointment to soften your skin and prevent ingrown hair. Please do not apply any moisturisers or lotions on the day of your treatment. If you are having under arm waxing, try to use a deodorant with anti-perspirant this allows the hair to be removed easily. Bath or shower on the day prior to your treatment, to open up follicles allowing easy hair removal.

3. How old must I be to have this done?
A. From age of 16+ you can receive this service.

4. How long does my hair need to be?
A. For an easy and safe waxing treatment, the hair must be ¼ inches long. If the hair is too short it will be difficult to remove, also if you are shaving for a method of quick hair removal please allow 10 days for regrowth to do a waxing treatment.

5. How long do waxing results last?
A. The results will vary person to person, and on your hair growth cycle. Typically, you can expect the results to last anywhere between 3-5 weeks. You will see a longer lasting effect with with repeat treatments. We recommend waxing treatments to be done every 4 weeks.

6. Will my hair re-growth be thicker with waxing treatments?
A. No, with consistent waxing treatments you will notice your hair re-growth to become finer and slower.

7. I’m pregnant, is this suitable for me?
A. Yes, you can have waxing treatments done. If you are having an intimate waxing treatment, we recommend you have this done after your first trimester.

8. Is waxing safe for sensitive skin?
A. Waxing is much gentler on the skin compared to other methods such as shaving. If you have sensitive skin, it’s very important to follow the pre/aftercare instructions provided. We also do laser hair removal as another safe option, which also lasts longer between 3-4 years with maintenance, why not speak to our technicians for further advice!

9. I have the following conditions/taking medication or have had treatments done, is this suitable for me?

  • Active herpes outbreak
  • Sunburn on the area to be treated
  • Chemical peel within the last 10 days
  • Use of retinol on the area to be treated within last 3 days
  • Acne medication such as Accutane or within the last 6 months
  • Eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis or varicose veins in the area to be treated
Unfortunately, we will not be able to carry out your waxing treatment with the above.

10. What should I be doing after my treatment, are there any restrictions? Waxing aftercare
A. We recommend you wear clean, lose fit clothing after your waxing treatment, and apply the products of which your therapist will let you know about.

For at least 24 hours after your treatment, avoid perfumed products/deodorants, direct sunlight/fake tan, chlorinated water, steam, make up, rubbing or scratching the area as your skin will be highly sensitive. Avoid exercising for 24 hours as sweating may cause the skin to be itchy. Exposure mentioned can cause irritation and inflammation. If any irritation occurs apply a cool damp compress to the area, if these symptoms persist more than 24 hours, seek medical advice.

1. How is Waxing done?
A. Waxing is done by using two types of methods, which is warm wax and hot wax. A paper strip is applied and pressed firmly, adhering the strip to the wax it is then pulled off against the direction of the hair growth. The hot wax method is done the same way, but without the use of a paper strips, the wax is self-setting and pulled off against the direction of the hair growth.

2. Is there anything I need to do before my waxing treatment? Waxing Pre-care?
A. We recommend you exfoliate the day before your waxing appointment to soften your skin and prevent ingrown hair. Please do not apply any moisturisers or lotions on the day of your treatment. If you are having under arm waxing, try to use a deodorant with anti-perspirant this allows the hair to be removed easily. Bath or shower on the day prior to your treatment, to open up follicles allowing easy hair removal.

3. How old must I be to have this done?
A. From age of 16+ you can receive this service.

4. How long does my hair need to be?
A. For an easy and safe waxing treatment, the hair must be ¼ inches long. If the hair is too short it will be difficult to remove, also if you are shaving for a method of quick hair removal please allow 10 days for regrowth to do a waxing treatment.

5. How long do waxing results last?
A. The results will vary person to person, and on your hair growth cycle. Typically, you can expect the results to last anywhere between 3-5 weeks. You will see a longer lasting effect with with repeat treatments. We recommend waxing treatments to be done every 4 weeks.

6. Will my hair re-growth be thicker with waxing treatments?
A. No, with consistent waxing treatments you will notice your hair re-growth to become finer and slower.

7. I’m pregnant, is this suitable for me?
A. Yes, you can have waxing treatments done. If you are having an intimate waxing treatment, we recommend you have this done after your first trimester.

8. Is waxing safe for sensitive skin?
A. Waxing is much gentler on the skin compared to other methods such as shaving. If you have sensitive skin, it’s very important to follow the pre/aftercare instructions provided. We also do laser hair removal as another safe option, which also lasts longer between 3-4 years with maintenance, why not speak to our technicians for further advice!

9. I have the following conditions/taking medication or have had treatments done, is this suitable for me?
A.
  • Active herpes outbreak
  • Sunburn on the area to be treated
  • Chemical peel within the last 10 days
  • Use of retinol on the area to be treated within last 3 days
  • Acne medication such as Accutane or within the last 6 months
  • Eczema, psoriasis, dermatitis or varicose veins in the area to be treated
Unfortunately, we will not be able to carry out your waxing treatment with the above.

10. What should I be doing after my treatment, are there any restrictions? Waxing aftercare
A. We recommend you wear clean, lose fit clothing after your waxing treatment, and apply the products of which your therapist will let you know about.

For at least 24 hours after your treatment, avoid perfumed products/deodorants, direct sunlight/fake tan, chlorinated water, steam, make up, rubbing or scratching the area as your skin will be highly sensitive. Avoid exercising for 24 hours as sweating may cause the skin to be itchy. Exposure mentioned can cause irritation and inflammation. If any irritation occurs apply a cool damp compress to the area, if these symptoms persist more than 24 hours, seek medical advice.
